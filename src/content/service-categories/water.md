Water-heater repair, water-heater installation, and tankless installs / conversions — handled end-to-end. Most contractors split this work across a plumber and a gasfitter (or a plumber and a sub-contracted gasfitter). Halo Air does both, and the warranty is signed by one company.

## What "fully installed" means here

Most water-heater quotes leave out half the actual job. A proper install quote includes the equipment itself, removal and disposal of the old unit, all new fittings (sometimes including expansion tank and dielectric unions, both required by current Alberta code), gas-line connection with leak test, venting modifications (often required when swapping tank types), pressure-relief valve discharge piping, and a permit pulled with your municipality. If a quote skips any of these, you're getting a teaser — ask for a written itemized estimate.

Halo Air installs include all of it, in writing, before any work starts.

## When a quick anode swap saves a whole tank

The single highest-ROI water-heater maintenance task in Alberta is the **anode rod swap** around year 5–6 of a tank's life. The anode is a sacrificial magnesium or aluminum rod that corrodes in place of the steel tank itself. Once it's gone (and most homeowners never even check), the tank starts corroding from the inside, and you've shortened the unit's life by 3–5 years. Most tank failures we see in homes built before 2018 trace back to a never-replaced anode.

If your tank is 5+ years old and you've never had the anode checked, ask about it on your next service call. It's a 30-minute job most of the time and one of the cheapest interventions on any HVAC equipment in your home.
