## Sherwood Park HVAC, plumbing and gas — the local view

Sherwood Park's housing stock skews newer than most of Edmonton — a lot of homes built in the 1990s and 2000s — which means we see a lot of mid-life furnace replacements and matched AC retrofits right now. The newer build envelopes in places like Heritage Hills and Lakeland Ridge are great fit for high-efficiency furnace + AC upgrades; older Mills Haven and Granada homes usually benefit from a high-efficiency furnace upgrade with proper duct re-balancing.

### Neighbourhoods we know

- **Broadmoor & Mills Haven** — mature trees, mature homes, mature furnaces. Lots of 18–22 year old units approaching end-of-life. We've replaced dozens here in the last few years.
- **Heritage Hills & Lakeland Ridge** — newer construction, tighter envelopes, big square footage. AC retrofits land in this band.
- **Foxboro, Salisbury Village** — established suburban grid, mostly 1990s. Typical jobs are 15-year furnace replacement + first AC install.
- **Granada, Glen Allan** — older subdivisions with character homes; sometimes interesting duct routings that need a thoughtful install crew.

## Strathcona County permits — the short version

Sherwood Park is part of Strathcona County, not the City of Edmonton, so permits go through the County's Building Services group (not the City portal). The practical difference for homeowners:

- Turnaround tends to be **faster** in Strathcona County than in Edmonton (often 1–2 business days for a routine furnace permit vs. 2–3 in Edmonton).
- Inspections are typically scheduled within a week of completion.
- The County's permit fee structure is similar to Edmonton's for residential HVAC.

We handle all of this end-to-end. Your written install quote includes a permit line-item, we file on your behalf, and we coordinate the County inspector visit. You get the green tag in your hands at the end.
