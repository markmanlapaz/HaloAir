### Neighbourhood notes

We get a lot of calls from these areas in particular and have a feel for what's typical in each:

- **Riverbend, Terwillegar, Windermere** — newer high-efficiency furnaces, often original to the build. Condensate drain issues are common around year 8–10.
- **Mill Woods, Millbourne** — wide range of build ages from 1970s to current. The older Mill Woods homes are great candidates for full system replacement; modern equipment can shave 30%+ off the gas bill.
- **Strathearn, Bonnie Doon, Garneau, Westmount** — older mature homes, often with retrofitted ductwork or hybrid boiler + forced-air setups. We're comfortable in these — they need a thoughtful tradesperson, not a "cookie-cutter" replacement crew.
- **Glenora, Crestwood, Laurier Heights** — heritage character homes with chimney venting and natural-draft equipment. Combustion safety testing matters here; we always run a calibrated CO analyzer.
- **Belgravia, McKernan, Allendale** — mature homes near the university. Often dual furnaces, finished basements, retrofitted AC. We're set up to navigate the access challenges these jobs come with.

## Edmonton permits & rules — what you should know

The City of Edmonton's Development Services group requires permits for most HVAC equipment swaps. The good news: as a journeyman-licensed contractor we pull them automatically and the City's online system is fast (most furnace permits are issued within 1–3 business days). Specifically:

- **Building Permit** is needed for furnace replacement when venting, gas line routing, or combustion-air setup changes.
- **Gas Permit** is triggered by any new gas appliance connection, gas line modification over 6 metres, or pressure changes.

Every install quote we send for an Edmonton job includes the full permit line-item up front. We file, we book the City inspector visit, we hand you the green tag when it's done.
