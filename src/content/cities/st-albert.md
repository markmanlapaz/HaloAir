## St. Albert HVAC — the local view

St. Albert's a varied housing market: mature character homes in Mission and Forest Lawn from the 1950s–70s, established subdivisions in Lacombe Park and Akinsdale, plus rapidly growing newer areas like Erin Ridge and Jensen Lakes. Each comes with its own typical HVAC profile, and we adjust the diagnostic approach accordingly.

The mature areas south of St. Albert Trail — Mission, Sturgeon, Forest Lawn — usually benefit more from high-efficiency furnace replacements combined with thoughtful duct re-balancing. Lacombe Park sits in between: a mix of 1980s and 1990s homes where we see a lot of mid-life furnace and AC replacements right now.

## St. Albert permits

City of St. Albert handles its own permits independently of Edmonton and Strathcona County. The Planning and Engineering office is the right contact for HVAC, gas, and electrical permits. Most furnace and AC installs in St. Albert require a Building Permit; new gas appliance work needs a Gas Permit. Turnaround is typically 1–3 business days for residential work.

As with every Halo Air install: permits are part of the quote, we file them, we coordinate inspections, you get the green tag.
