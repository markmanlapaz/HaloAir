## When to call us right away

Some issues can wait until Monday. Others can't. Call **780-224-0024** as soon as it's safe to do so if:

- **You smell gas** — even faintly. Leave the building first, call from outside.
- **A CO detector is alarming** — leave the building. Call 911 if anyone has symptoms (headache, dizziness, nausea). Call us once everyone is safe.
- **No heat with outdoor temps below −15°C** — Alberta cold drops fast. A house can lose enough heat to freeze pipes in 8–12 hours below −20°C.
- **No heat with infants, elderly, or medically vulnerable residents** — at any temperature.
- **Water is actively leaking** from HVAC equipment, a burst pipe, or a failed water heater.
- **AC out during an extreme heat advisory** with elderly residents.

If you're not sure whether your situation qualifies, **just call or email**. The conversation is free, and we'd rather hear from you than have you wait it out.

## How an after-hours visit works

1. **Triage.** When you call or email, we ask safety questions first, fault questions second. We tell you what to do to stay safe and give an honest ETA.
2. **Dispatch.** We come stocked for the reported issue — no-heat calls bring furnace board / igniter / inducer parts; no-cool calls bring capacitor / contactor / refrigerant; gas calls bring the leak detector.
3. **Arrival.** Marked vehicle, ID visible. We walk you through the diagnosis as we work.
4. **Written estimate.** You see the quote in writing before any work starts. After-hours fees, if any, are disclosed up front.
5. **The work.** Most no-heat and no-cool issues are resolved in the same visit. Heavy parts (compressors, full furnace replacements) need order time — we'll help bridge the gap (space heaters, hotel-night reimbursement where applicable) for total-loss situations.
6. **Sign-off.** Test under load, document the work, leave the parts box, file the warranty entry, send the invoice by email.

## What we can handle outside business hours

**Heating** — failed furnace, no ignition, blower not running, CO alarm, gas valve failure, control board fault, condensate drain flooding the basement.

**Cooling** — no-cool, compressor lockout, refrigerant leak, frozen coil, fan motor failure, electrical fault tripping the breaker.

**Gas** — leak detection and repair, gas appliance shutdown, pressure regulator failure, BBQ / fireplace / dryer disconnection.

**Water heaters & plumbing** — burst pipe shutoff, water heater failure (tank or tankless), main shut-off issues, leaking valves, frozen pipe thawing.
