## Why annual furnace maintenance pays for itself

Most furnace failures are not random. A capacitor that fails in November was usually weak in September. A heat exchanger that cracks in January was usually showing soot patterns in October. A condensate drain that floods in February was usually showing biofilm in May. The point of an annual tune-up isn't to "look at the furnace" — it's to **catch developing problems while they're still cheap to fix**.

The other half of the case for maintenance is **warranty preservation**. Almost every major HVAC manufacturer requires documented annual service to honour their 10-year parts warranty. If your inducer motor fails in year 8 and you don't have a service record, you're paying out of pocket. An annual visit is cheap insurance against that.

## What our fall furnace tune-up covers (23 points)

**Combustion & safety**
- Burner inspection and cleaning
- Heat exchanger visual + combustion analyzer test for cracks
- Flame sensor cleaning
- Gas pressure verification (manifold + inlet)
- Carbon monoxide measurement at supply and return
- Flue draft and venting check
- High-limit safety switch test

**Electrical & controls**
- Capacitor microfarad test
- Blower motor amperage draw
- Inducer motor inspection
- Control board diagnostics
- Thermostat calibration and battery check

**Airflow & comfort**
- Air filter inspection (replaced if needed)
- Blower wheel cleaning if accessible
- Static pressure measurement
- Supply / return temperature differential

**Plumbing & drain**
- Condensate drain flush (high-efficiency units)
- Condensate pump test (if equipped)
- Trap clean-out

**Written deliverable**
- Photo-documented condition report
- Recommended-action list with priority levels
- Filed service log entry (warranty preservation)

## When to book — and why fall

Best time to book a furnace tune-up is **September** — before the first cold snap loads the schedule. By mid-October the heating-season repair calls start crowding the dispatch board, and tune-up slots get pushed into November.

If you've never had your furnace serviced and it's over 5 years old, this is the most important call you'll make this year. If you bought a home in the last 18 months and don't know the service history, same answer.
