## What we fix on furnace repair calls

Edmonton furnaces fail in surprisingly predictable patterns, and the symptom usually narrows the diagnosis fast. The top calls we see in winter:

### Common failures, by symptom

- **No heat at all** — most often a failed igniter (hot-surface or spark), flame sensor coated in soot, or pressure switch stuck open. Same-visit fix in roughly 70% of cases.
- **Furnace starts, then shuts down after a minute** — flame sensor fault or a high-limit safety tripping. Cleaning the sensor takes 20 minutes; tracking down a high-limit trip takes longer (it's usually low airflow from a dirty filter or blocked vent).
- **Furnace blowing cold air** — gas valve fault, ignition failure, or a thermostat wiring issue. We test each electrically before pulling parts.
- **Loud bang on startup** — delayed ignition, often caused by dirty burners letting gas accumulate before lighting. Untreated it can crack the heat exchanger; we clean and re-test combustion.
- **Squealing or grinding** — blower motor bearings or a loose belt (on older units). Repair often beats replacement of a 12-year-old motor for $400–$650.
- **Furnace runs constantly but the house won't warm** — undersized or duct-leakage issue more often than a furnace fault. We measure static pressure and supply/return temperatures before recommending parts.

## The honest case for proper diagnostics

A lot of furnace "repair" in the trade is parts-throwing — replace the igniter and see if the symptom goes away, replace the gas valve and see if it stays on, replace the control board if the first two don't fix it. That works eventually but ends up costing the homeowner triple. Every Halo Air repair starts with the same calibrated tests in the same order so we identify the actual failure before we order parts.

## When repair stops making sense

If your furnace is older than 15 years, has had two or more major repairs in the past two seasons, shows soot patterns on the heat exchanger, or a CO measurement above 50 ppm in supply air — replacement is the safer and cheaper-over-lifetime call.
