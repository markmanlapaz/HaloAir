## What we fix on water-heater repair calls

Water heaters fail in predictable ways, and most are fixable for a fraction of replacement cost when caught early. Common Halo Air water-heater repair calls:

- **No hot water on a gas tank** — usually a failed thermocouple, pilot/igniter issue, or a tripped high-limit. Often a same-visit fix.
- **Lukewarm water or running out quickly** — sediment buildup, failed dip tube, or an aged anode rod. Flushing + anode replacement adds years of life to a tank that otherwise looked done.
- **Leaking water heater** — fitting/valve leaks are repairable; tank-body leaks are not (and mean replacement is overdue). Either way, we contain it fast.
- **Pilot won't stay lit** — thermocouple, gas valve, or venting/draft issue. We test each in order so you're not paying for parts you don't need.
- **Tankless error codes** — vent blockage, scale buildup, gas pressure, or board faults. We descale and service all major tankless brands.
- **Banging or popping sounds from the tank** — sediment on the bottom of the tank. A full flush often quiets it down and extends life.
- **T&P valve dripping** — could be thermal expansion (needs an expansion tank, which is current Alberta code anyway), excess pressure, or a failed valve. We diagnose with a pressure gauge before swapping parts.

## When repair stops making sense

A few situations where we'll honestly recommend [replacement](/services/water/water-heater-installation/) instead of repair:

- **Tank body leaking** (not a fitting) — the tank shell has corroded through; it's done
- **Tank 12+ years old** with any major component failure — labour + parts on an aging tank doesn't pay back
- **Pilot light keeps relighting issues** combined with a 10+ year old tank — usually points to multiple aging components
- **Yellow/orange burner flame** that doesn't clean up after a burner service — could be heat-exchanger contamination
- **Frequent T&P valve discharge** on a unit without an expansion tank — adding the expansion tank fixes it, but if the unit is 10+ years old, often worth the swap
