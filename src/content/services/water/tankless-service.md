## Why tankless maintenance actually matters

Tankless units are built to run for 18–22 years — but that lifespan assumes annual maintenance. Skip it and the failure path is predictable:

1. **Year 1–2 of skipped maintenance:** scale builds inside the heat exchanger. Flow rate drops a little. You probably don't notice.
2. **Year 3–4:** the unit works harder to hit setpoint. Gas consumption creeps up. Error codes start appearing — usually flame or flow related.
3. **Year 5+:** the heat exchanger fails. That's a $1,500–$2,500 part on most brands. Manufacturer denies the warranty claim because there's no maintenance record.

A 60-minute annual descale prevents all of that. It's the single highest-ROI maintenance call on any HVAC equipment in your home.

## What a maintenance visit looks like

We bring the descale pump and supplies. Plan on 60–90 minutes for a thorough job:

- **Isolate the unit** at the service valves (these are why tankless installs need proper service valves — without them, descaling is an hours-long ordeal).
- **Circulate descaler** through the heat exchanger for 30–45 minutes. Edmonton water leaves a noticeable amount of scale by year one.
- **Flush with fresh water** until the discharge runs clear and pH-neutral.
- **Clean inlet filter screen** — debris from the supply line collects here.
- **Inspect condensate path** — trap, neutralizer media, drain line. Acidic condensate without a working neutralizer eats concrete and copper.
- **Verify gas pressure** at the unit's inlet under full firing rate. Low pressure here is the most common cause of flow complaints and intermittent error codes.
- **Vent inspection** — sealed-combustion intake and exhaust, terminations, condensation traps in horizontal runs.
- **Run under load** — flow-rate test against the unit's rated GPM, temperature-rise verification.
- **Written report** for warranty file.

## Common tankless repair calls

- **Scale-related error codes** (E11, E12, error codes vary by brand) — descale on the visit, usually resolves the same day.
- **Ignition failures** — flame sensor cleaning, igniter replacement, or gas valve diagnosis.
- **Vent blockages** — bird nests, ice plugs in winter terminations, debris in horizontal runs.
- **Low gas pressure under load** — regulator adjustment or upstream gas-line issues (a Gasfitter ticket is required for this work; we hold one).
- **Flow sensor failure** — common after 8–10 years; the unit thinks no water is moving and won't fire.
- **Condensate-trap issues** — clogged trap, full neutralizer media, frozen drain line in unconditioned spaces.
- **PCB / control board faults** — rare but real; brand-specific replacement parts.

## When repair stops making sense

If a tankless unit is 15+ years old and the heat exchanger has failed (not a sensor, not a valve — the heat exchanger itself), [replacement](/services/water/tankless-water-heaters/) is usually the right call. A new heat exchanger is often 60–70% of the cost of a new unit, and you'd be installing the most failure-prone component in an otherwise aging assembly.

For everything else — sensors, valves, ignition, venting, descaling — repair is almost always the right answer.
