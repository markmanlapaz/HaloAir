## When tank replacement is the right call

A few clear signs your tank is done:

- **Tank-body leaking** (not a fitting) — the steel shell has corroded through. It's not fixable. Replace before it fails entirely and floods a basement.
- **Tank over 10 years old** with rising bills — efficiency degrades over time. A modern high-efficiency tank can shave 15–25% off heating costs on the gas side.
- **Rust-coloured hot water** — internal corrosion. Usually means the anode rod is long gone and the tank lining is next.
- **Lukewarm water across the whole house** — sediment + aging element. On older tanks, not worth a full flush + element swap.
- **Two or more major repairs in 24 months** — replacement wins on lifetime cost.

If you're between repair and replace, our [water heater repair](/services/water/water-heater-repair/) page covers the call where you'd want a diagnostic first.

## What we install

**Standard atmospheric-vent gas tanks** (40 / 50 / 60-gallon) — the workhorse for most Edmonton homes. Vents through an existing B-vent chimney shared with the furnace, no electrical needed beyond a 120V control circuit. Brands: A.O. Smith, Bradford White, Rheem, John Wood.

**Power-vent gas tanks** — for homes without atmospheric venting available, or where you want venting flexibility (sidewall instead of up the chimney). Plug-in electrical for the vent fan. ~15–20% better efficiency than atmospheric vent.

**Tankless / on-demand systems** — separate page, separate considerations. See [tankless water heaters](/services/water/tankless-water-heaters/).

## What an install quote includes

- **The water heater itself** (equipment cost)
- **Removal and proper disposal** of the old unit
- **All new fittings** including dielectric unions where required by code
- **Gas line work** — new connection, sediment trap, drip leg, pressure test
- **Venting modifications** as required (B-vent or sidewall PVC)
- **Expansion tank** — required by current Alberta code on any closed-loop system
- **T&P valve discharge piping** routed to a drain or outside per code
- **Seismic strap** where applicable
- **Municipal permit** pulled with City of Edmonton (or your municipality's equivalent) and filed on your behalf
- **Commissioning** under load with combustion analyzer reading (for atmospheric vents) or vent draft verification (for power-vents)
- **Customer walkthrough** with shutoffs, temperature setting, maintenance schedule

## The case for adding an expansion tank during the swap

Even if your current tank doesn't have one, current Alberta code requires an expansion tank on any new water heater install in a closed-loop plumbing system (which is nearly every home with a backflow preventer at the meter — and they're almost universal in Edmonton). Skipping it means thermal-expansion pressure surges every time the burner fires, which prematurely wears the T&P valve and tank itself. We include it on every install at no upsell.
