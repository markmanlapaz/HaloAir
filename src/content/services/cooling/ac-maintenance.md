## Why annual AC maintenance pays for itself

A dirty AC coil drops efficiency 15–25%. A weak capacitor lets a compressor draw more current than it should — once per cycle, every time, for months — until the compressor windings fail. A blocked condensate drain floods a basement on the first humid week of July. Each of these is caught in a 60-minute spring tune-up; each costs significantly more to repair as an emergency call.

The other half of the case is **warranty preservation**. Most AC manufacturers require documented annual service to honour their 10-year parts warranty. If your compressor fails in year 8 and there's no service log, you're paying out of pocket.

## What our spring AC tune-up covers (18 points)

**Refrigerant**
- High-side and low-side pressure measurement
- Superheat calculation (vs. manufacturer target)
- Subcool calculation
- Refrigerant line insulation check
- Flare fitting visual + electronic leak detector spot-check

**Electrical & controls**
- Capacitor microfarad test (against rated value)
- Contactor inspection and amperage draw
- Condenser fan motor amperage
- Electrical disconnect and breaker inspection
- Thermostat cooling-mode calibration

**Coils & airflow**
- Outdoor condenser coil cleaning (water + coil-safe cleaner)
- Indoor evaporator coil visual inspection
- Blower wheel and filter inspection
- Static pressure measurement
- Supply-air / return-air temperature differential

**Condensate**
- Drain pan inspection
- Drain line flush
- Condensate pump test (if equipped)

**Written deliverable**
- Photo-documented condition report
- Recommended-action list with priority levels
- Filed service log entry (warranty preservation)

## When to book — and why spring

Best time to book an AC tune-up is **April or early May** — before the first 25°C+ stretch loads the dispatch board. By mid-June we're typically booked 2–3 weeks out. A spring service finds problems while they're still cheap to fix and gives you a clean start to the cooling season.

If you've never had your AC serviced and it's over 5 years old, this is the most important call you'll make this spring. If you bought a home in the last 18 months and don't know the service history, same answer.
