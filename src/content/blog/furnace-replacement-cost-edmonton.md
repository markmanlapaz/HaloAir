## TL;DR

Replacing a furnace in an Edmonton home is a multi-trade job, not just dropping in a box. The right tier depends on your home's comfort profile, ductwork, and how long you plan to stay. Every Halo Air quote is written, itemized, and given before any work starts — so you see exactly what's included and why. The cheapest path almost never beats lifetime cost.

## What "fully installed" actually includes

Most quotes you'll see online are equipment-only or "labour" estimates that don't account for the real work. A complete furnace replacement quote should include:

- The furnace itself (equipment cost)
- Removal and proper disposal of the old furnace
- Venting changes (if any) and combustion-air verification
- Gas line modifications (if any) and pressure-testing
- New thermostat (programmable or smart, your choice)
- City of Edmonton (or local municipality) permits — Building Permit, and Gas Permit if applicable
- Permit inspection coordination
- Combustion analysis with calibrated analyzer
- Warranty registration filed on your behalf
- Customer walkthrough + maintenance briefing

If a quote is missing any of these line items, it's not a real quote — it's a teaser to get in the door. Ask for a written, itemized estimate.

## A note on Canadian efficiency rules

Federal Energy Efficiency Regulations now require new residential gas furnaces sold in Canada to be **at least 95% AFUE** and use a **multi-stage (ECM) blower**. Older 80% AFUE single-stage units are no longer a legal new-install option for a typical Edmonton home, so every quote we write today is for a modern condensing unit. The good news: the regulated floor is genuinely better equipment — quieter, more efficient, and longer-lived than what was being sold a decade ago.

## 2026 Edmonton furnace tiers

We quote three tiers plus a combined-system option. Pricing varies by home, equipment selected, and the five variables below — every quote is written and itemized before any work starts.

### Mid tier: 96% AFUE single-stage
The sweet spot for most Edmonton homes. Modern condensing furnace with multi-stage ECM blower, full Energy Star certification, 10-year manufacturer warranty. Reliable, efficient, and meets all current Canadian efficiency requirements.

### Mid-premium tier: 96% AFUE two-stage
Two heat output stages — runs at low fire most of the time and high fire only when needed. Quieter, more even temperatures, slightly better efficiency. Worth it in homes with comfort issues (rooms that run hot/cold).

### Premium tier: 96%+ AFUE modulating + ECM blower
Variable-output furnace with a precision blower. Best comfort, lowest noise, highest efficiency. Worth it in higher-end homes or where the homeowner values comfort precision.

### Combo: 96% AFUE furnace + matched AC
Replacing both at once shares labour, permits, and electrical work, so the combined quote is meaningfully less than the two installs done separately. The best value play for homeowners who'd be due for an AC replacement within the next few years anyway.

## Five things that move the number

1. **Existing venting compatibility.** If we can reuse your current PVC venting, great. If we need to re-route or add a sidewall vent, that adds to the scope.
2. **Gas line length and sizing.** Moving a furnace location, or upsizing a line to feed a larger BTU furnace, adds time and materials.
3. **Ductwork condition.** Bad transitions, undersized returns, leaky ducts — all common in older Edmonton homes. Replace-and-reseal work varies by scope.
4. **Thermostat upgrade.** A standard programmable thermostat is included in most quotes. A premium smart thermostat (Ecobee SmartThermostat Premium, Nest Learning) is an upgrade option.
5. **Combined replacement.** Replacing the AC (or water heater) at the same time saves money vs. doing them separately because labour, permits, and venting work overlap.

## What we recommend asking every contractor

- "Is this a written, itemized quote with permits included?"
- "Will you do a Manual J load calculation before recommending a furnace size?"
- "Do you pull permits or expect me to?"
- "Is gas-line work, combustion testing, and warranty registration included?"
- "What's the labour warranty in addition to the manufacturer warranty?"

A contractor who hesitates on any of these is showing you what they'd skip on the actual install. Walk.
