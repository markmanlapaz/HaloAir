## TL;DR

A high-efficiency mid-tier furnace install in an average Edmonton home runs **$5,500–$8,500** fully installed in 2026 (equipment, labour, permits, removal of old unit). Premium two-stage and modulating units run **$8,500–$12,000**. The number moves based on five variables — listed below. The cheapest path almost never beats lifetime cost.

## What "fully installed" actually includes

Most quotes you'll see online are equipment-only or "labour" estimates that don't account for the real work. A complete furnace replacement quote should include:

- The furnace itself (equipment cost)
- Removal and proper disposal of the old furnace
- Venting changes (if any) and combustion-air verification
- Gas line modifications (if any) and pressure-testing
- New thermostat (programmable or smart, your choice)
- City of Edmonton (or local municipality) permits — Building Permit, and Gas Permit if applicable
- Permit inspection coordination
- Combustion analysis with calibrated analyzer
- Warranty registration filed on your behalf
- Customer walkthrough + maintenance briefing

If a quote is missing any of these line items, it's not a real quote — it's a teaser to get in the door. Ask for a written, itemized estimate.

## 2026 Edmonton pricing by tier

### Entry tier: 80% AFUE single-stage — $3,800–$4,500
Old technology, but technically available. Saves money up front and that's it. Annual gas bill will be 20–30% higher than a modern high-efficiency unit. We only quote this when there's a specific reason (e.g. a rental property the owner intends to sell).

### Mid tier: 96% AFUE single-stage — $5,500–$7,500
The sweet spot for most Edmonton homes. Modern condensing furnace, full Energy Star certification, 10-year manufacturer warranty. Pays back the cost difference vs. the entry tier within 4–5 years.

### Mid-premium tier: 96% AFUE two-stage — $7,500–$9,500
Two heat output stages — runs at low fire most of the time and high fire only when needed. Quieter, more even temperatures, slightly better efficiency. Worth it in homes with comfort issues (rooms that run hot/cold).

### Premium tier: 96%+ AFUE modulating + ECM blower — $9,500–$12,000
Variable-output furnace with a precision-blower. Best comfort, lowest noise, highest efficiency. Worth it in higher-end homes or where the homeowner values comfort precision.

### Combo: 96% AFUE furnace + matched AC — $9,500–$14,000
Replacing both at once shares labour, permits, and electrical work, so the combined price is meaningfully less than the two installs done separately. The best value play for homeowners who'd be due for an AC replacement within the next few years anyway.

## Five things that move the number

1. **Existing venting compatibility.** If we can reuse your current PVC venting, great. If we need to re-route or add a sidewall vent, add $400–$900.
2. **Gas line length and sizing.** Moving a furnace location, or upsizing a line to feed a larger BTU furnace, adds $300–$1,200.
3. **Ductwork condition.** Bad transitions, undersized returns, leaky ducts — all common in older Edmonton homes. Replace-and-reseal work adds $500–$2,500 depending on scope.
4. **Thermostat upgrade.** Standard programmable thermostat is included in most quotes. Premium smart thermostat (Ecobee SmartThermostat Premium, Nest Learning) adds $250–$400.
5. **Combined replacement.** Replacing the AC (or water heater) at the same time saves money vs. doing them separately because labour, permits, and venting work overlap.

## What we recommend asking every contractor

- "Is this a written, itemized quote with permits included?"
- "Will you do a Manual J load calculation before recommending a furnace size?"
- "Do you pull permits or expect me to?"
- "Is gas-line work, combustion testing, and warranty registration included?"
- "What's the labour warranty in addition to the manufacturer warranty?"

A contractor who hesitates on any of these is showing you what they'd skip on the actual install. Walk.
