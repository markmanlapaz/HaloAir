## TL;DR

The 8 signs your furnace is failing: **(1) age over 15 years**, **(2) rising gas bills with no other changes**, **(3) yellow flame instead of blue**, **(4) frequent short-cycling**, **(5) uneven heat between rooms**, **(6) unusual noises**, **(7) more dust than usual**, **(8) CO detector alarm**. One sign on its own is often a simple repair. Two or more = budget for replacement.

## The 8 signs in detail

### 1. Age over 15 years

Edmonton furnaces typically run 15–20 years before earning replacement. The reliability curve doesn't drop off slowly — it falls off a cliff somewhere between year 16 and year 22 for most units. If yours is over 15, you're inside the danger window.

### 2. Rising gas bills with no other changes

If you haven't changed thermostat habits, the home hasn't expanded, and you're using gas the same way as last winter — but your bill is climbing — your furnace's efficiency is degrading. A 10-year-old furnace losing 5–10% efficiency is normal. A 15-year-old furnace losing 20%+ is replacement territory.

### 3. Yellow flame instead of blue

Open the panel (carefully) and look at the burner flame. A healthy gas flame is **blue with a small yellow tip**. A flame that's primarily yellow or orange means **incomplete combustion** — and incomplete combustion produces carbon monoxide. Stop using the furnace until it's been inspected by a licensed technician.

### 4. Frequent short-cycling

If your furnace runs for 2–5 minutes, shuts off, runs again 10 minutes later, shuts off — that's short-cycling, and it's bad. Common causes: oversized furnace (was wrong from day one), failing flame sensor, dirty filter restricting airflow, faulty high-limit switch, or a cracked heat exchanger. Some are cheap fixes. Some are end-of-life.

### 5. Uneven heat between rooms

If certain rooms have always run cold, that's a ductwork or design issue. But if rooms that *used* to heat fine now run cold, your furnace is losing the capacity to heat the whole envelope properly. Combined with high bills, this almost always means it's time.

### 6. Unusual noises

A healthy furnace makes a steady whoosh when running and clicks at startup/shutdown. Sounds you should NOT hear:
- **Booming** at startup — delayed ignition, often a dirty burner. Repair cost low; safety risk medium.
- **Grinding** — failed blower motor bearings. Usually a motor replacement.
- **Squealing** — failed blower belt (on older units) or bad motor bearings.
- **Banging in the ductwork** — duct expansion/contraction. Usually a fix, not a furnace issue.
- **Constant clicking** — failed ignitor or flame sensor. Common, cheap fix.

### 7. More dust than usual

A healthy furnace + filter system keeps household dust low. If you're suddenly dusting twice as often, your filter may be improperly sealed, your blower may be developing issues, or your ductwork may have a leak letting dirty air bypass the filter. Some of these are repair-level; combined with age, they suggest broader system fatigue.

### 8. CO detector alarm

If your CO detector goes off:
1. **Open windows** to ventilate
2. **Leave the building**
3. **Call 911** if anyone has symptoms (headache, dizziness, nausea, confusion)
4. **Once everyone is safe**, call us at 780-224-0024

A CO alarm caused by your furnace usually means a **cracked heat exchanger** — and that's a replacement situation, not a repair. Heat exchangers leak combustion gases into the home's air supply, which is exactly what you don't want.

## What to do once you see two or more signs

1. **Book a diagnostic.** A 60–90 minute combustion analysis tells you exactly where the system is. Halo Air diagnostic fee is waived if you proceed with repair or replacement.
2. **Get a written quote for both paths.** Repair cost AND replacement cost, side-by-side. Apply the [30% rule](/blog/repair-vs-replace-ac/) — if repair exceeds 30% of replacement, lean replace.
3. **Don't wait for winter.** Furnace failures cluster on the coldest nights of the year. Replacing in shoulder season (May, September) gets faster install dates and avoids emergency-call premiums.
4. **Apply for rebates.** A qualifying high-efficiency furnace install can unlock federal and provincial rebates — programs change year to year, but as of 2026 there are several utility-led options. We file the paperwork for you as part of every install quote.
