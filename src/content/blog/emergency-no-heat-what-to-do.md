## TL;DR

If your furnace just died in cold weather:
1. **Call us at 780-224-0024** — after-hours service available on request
2. **Run a trickle of cold water** on every tap in the home (prevents frozen pipes)
3. **Gather everyone into one interior room**, close the door, close curtains
4. **Plug in space heaters** (electric only — NEVER use a gas oven for heat)
5. **Don't keep restarting the furnace** if it's failed multiple times — let us diagnose

## The first 30 minutes

### Minute 1: Call

Pick up the phone. Call **780-224-0024** — or email haloairhvac@gmail.com if it's outside business hours. We triage the situation and give you an honest ETA. If it's a verified life-safety situation (no-heat below −20°C, vulnerable household members), we prioritize.

### Minutes 2–5: Protect the pipes

Pipes freezing is the most expensive collateral damage of a furnace failure. The fix is simple: **run a trickle of cold water** on every tap in the home, including basement laundry sinks and seldom-used taps. Moving water doesn't freeze. Trickle = about the diameter of a pencil lead.

Special attention:
- Kitchen sink on an exterior wall
- Basement laundry (exposed copper/PEX in cold air)
- Bathroom sinks on exterior walls
- Outdoor hose bibs (if not winterized) — shut them off at the indoor valve

### Minutes 5–10: Concentrate the heat

You can't keep a whole Edmonton home at 21°C with space heaters. You CAN keep one room at 18°C, which is fine for the duration of a service call.

- Pick an **interior room** with no exterior walls if possible (or fewest)
- Close the door
- Close all curtains and blinds
- Place towels along door bottoms to seal gaps
- Plug in **electric** space heaters (1500W each on dedicated circuits — never gas, never open-flame inside)
- Gather everyone into that room

### Minutes 10–30: Add humidity

Cold air is dry, and dry air feels colder than humid air at the same temperature. A small humidifier — or even a pot of warm tap water sitting open in your concentrated room — adds humidity that makes 18°C feel more like 20°C.

## What NOT to do

These are the mistakes we see most often on emergency calls. Don't.

- **Don't run a gas oven for heat.** CO buildup is lethal within hours, even with the door open. This kills people every winter in Alberta.
- **Don't use a BBQ, fire pit, or any combustion appliance indoors.** Same reason.
- **Don't restart the furnace more than twice.** If it failed once and the restart worked, fine. If it failed twice and the restart doesn't hold, leave it off and wait for diagnosis. Repeated restarts can flood the heat exchanger or damage the control board.
- **Don't open hot water taps full-blast.** Hot water travels through the same plumbing the cold lines use. Save the hot water tank for what you need.
- **Don't ignore CO detectors.** If a CO detector alarms, leave the building. Call 911 if anyone has symptoms.

## Special situations

### Gas smell

Stop reading. Leave the building immediately. Do **not** use light switches, phones, or anything electrical inside. Call us at **780-224-0024** AND ATCO at **1-800-511-3447** from outside.

### CO detector alarm

Open windows. Leave the building. Call 911 if anyone has symptoms (headache, dizziness, nausea, confusion). Once everyone is safe, call us.

### Frozen pipes already

If pipes have already frozen and you can't get water flowing:
- **Identify which lines are frozen** (no water flow at a specific tap)
- **Do NOT use an open flame to thaw** — this is the #1 cause of house fires in winter
- **Use a hair dryer or heat gun on LOW**, starting from the tap end and working back
- **Open the affected tap fully** so melt water can escape
- **If pipes have burst** (water spraying), shut off the main water valve and call us — we cover plumbing too

### Vulnerable residents

If you have infants, elderly residents, or anyone with medical conditions in the home, mention it during the dispatch call. We prioritize these situations.
